# Golden Retriever Summer Shedding: Why It Gets Worse & What Actually Helps

*By GoldenRetriever.hair*

**Golden Retriever summer shedding** can feel overwhelming. Loose fur on floors, couches, clothes, and car seats is common, especially when the weather warms up and the undercoat changes. For many owners, the question is not only *why is my Golden Retriever shedding so much* — it is also what actually helps without damaging the coat.

Shedding is usually normal for a Golden Retriever, but the right seasonal routine makes it much easier to manage. This guide focuses on warm-weather shedding, brushing habits, and practical seasonal care — not year-round shedding basics covered in our [Golden Retriever shedding guide](/guides/golden-retriever-shedding-guide).

This guide is educational only. If your Golden has bald patches, red or irritated skin, sores, sudden coat loss, itching, odor, or other concerning symptoms, contact your veterinarian.

## Quick Answer: Why Is My Golden Retriever Shedding So Much in Summer?

- Golden Retrievers have a **double coat**.
- Many Goldens shed year-round.
- Seasonal coat changes can make **golden retriever shedding in summer** feel heavier.
- Warm weather, daylight changes, brushing habits, bathing, health, diet, and indoor climate can all affect shedding.
- The goal is not to stop shedding completely; it is to manage loose fur and keep the coat healthy.
- Brushing more often usually helps more than brushing harder.

The short version: brush more consistently, use the right tool for the coat layer you are working on, bathe only when needed, dry the coat well, and watch for skin problems that are more than normal seasonal shedding.

## Is Summer Shedding Normal for Golden Retrievers?

Yes. Shedding is normal for this breed.

Golden Retrievers are heavy shedders because of their thick **golden retriever double coat**. Summer shedding often feels worse because the dog is losing more loose undercoat — the soft, dense layer many owners see in clumps on the brush or floor.

Some Goldens shed gradually through warm months. Others seem to **blow coat** more noticeably for a stretch, especially when undercoat comes out heavily. Indoor dogs may shed in less predictable patterns because of artificial lighting, air conditioning, and stable indoor temperatures.

If shedding is the only issue and the coat looks healthy, summer fur loss is often part of normal seasonal management — not a reason to panic.

## Understanding the Golden Retriever Double Coat

A Golden’s coat has two main layers:

- **Topcoat (guard hairs)** — coarser hairs that help protect against dirt, moisture, and everyday environmental exposure.
- **Undercoat** — softer, denser fur that adds insulation and is often what owners notice coming out in volume during heavier shedding periods.

Proper brushing helps remove loose undercoat before it spreads through the house. For coat type differences across lines and colors, see the [Golden Retriever coat types guide](/guides/golden-retriever-coat-types-guide).

## What Actually Helps With Summer Shedding?

### 1. Brush more consistently

Regular brushing is the biggest controllable factor for **how to reduce golden retriever shedding** at home. During heavier summer shedding, many Goldens need brushing several times per week. Some benefit from short daily sessions — five to ten minutes can remove a surprising amount of loose coat without turning grooming into a long, frustrating chore.

### 2. Use the right brush for the job

Different tools do different jobs:

- **Slicker brush** — helps lift loose coat and address surface tangles.
- **Undercoat rake or deshedding tool** — used carefully for loose undercoat; not for daily aggressive scraping.
- **Pin brush or comb** — useful for feathering and finishing after bulk undercoat is removed.

Do not overuse aggressive deshedding tools. For tool picks and how to use them, see [best brushes for Golden Retrievers](/guides/best-brushes-golden-retrievers).

### Helpful Brushes for Summer Shedding

During heavier summer shedding, the right brush combination matters more than brushing harder. A slicker brush helps with loose coat and surface tangles, while a grooming rake can help remove loose undercoat more efficiently when used gently.

### 3. Bathe when needed, but do not overdo it

Bathing can loosen dead coat and remove dirt that makes brushing harder. That said, overbathing or harsh shampoo can irritate skin and may make shedding *look* worse if the coat becomes dry or itchy.

Pair baths with brushing and a gentle shampoo suited for Goldens. See [best shampoo for Golden Retrievers](/guides/best-shampoo-golden-retrievers) for product guidance.

### Gentle Shampoo for Shedding Season Baths

Bathing can help loosen dead coat and make brushing more effective, but shampoo should be gentle enough for regular coat care and rinsed thoroughly.

### 4. Keep the coat dry and clean

Damp coat, trapped undercoat, and poor drying after swimming or baths can contribute to odor, mats, or skin irritation. Dry thoroughly, especially around feathering, the belly, and behind the ears.

### 5. Support skin and coat from the inside

Balanced nutrition and good hydration support coat quality. If shedding suddenly increases, the coat becomes dull or brittle, or skin changes appear, ask your veterinarian before assuming it is only seasonal. This guide does not recommend specific supplements.

## What Not to Do During Summer Shedding

- Do not shave a Golden Retriever just because it is summer.
- Do not brush so aggressively that the skin gets irritated.
- Do not use deshedding tools every day unless a groomer or veterinarian specifically recommends it.
- Do not ignore bald patches, scabs, odor, or excessive itching.
- Do not assume every coat change is seasonal without checking skin and overall health.

## Should You Shave a Golden Retriever in Summer?

In general, **should you shave a golden retriever in summer** as a routine shedding fix? No.

Shaving a Golden’s double coat is not recommended as a standard warm-weather solution:

- Shaving does not stop shedding — short hairs still shed.
- It can interfere with how the coat protects the skin.
- It may increase sun exposure and irritation risk.
- It will not replace brushing, undercoat removal, shade, hydration, or heat planning.

If mats are severe, a groomer or veterinarian may need to clip specific areas for health or comfort, but that is different from shaving the whole coat as a routine summer strategy.

Instead, focus on consistent brushing, gentle bathing when needed, and smart summer habits. For heat-specific safety, see [Golden Retriever heat safety](/guides/golden-retriever-heat-safety). For broader warm-weather care, see the [Golden Retriever summer care guide](/guides/golden-retriever-summer-care-guide).

## When Summer Shedding Might Be a Problem

Contact your veterinarian if you notice:

- bald patches
- red or inflamed skin
- strong odor
- constant scratching
- hot spots
- sudden coat thinning
- dull, brittle coat
- excessive licking or chewing
- skin flakes, sores, or scabs

These signs may point to allergies, infection, parasites, hormonal issues, or other conditions — not simple seasonal shedding. For common skin and coat concerns, read the [Golden Retriever skin problems guide](/guides/golden-retriever-skin-problems-guide).

## Simple Summer Shedding Routine

### Daily or near-daily during heavy shedding

- Quick 5–10 minute brushing session
- Check feathering behind ears, tail, legs, and belly
- Look for mats, burrs, skin irritation, or hot spots

### Weekly

- Deeper brushing session
- Comb and check feathering
- Clean grooming tools
- Check ears and paws

### As needed

- Bath with gentle dog shampoo
- Thorough drying
- Groomer visit if the coat is packed, matted, or hard to manage at home

## Best Areas to Brush During Summer Shedding

Focus on areas where undercoat and feathering collect:

- neck and shoulders
- chest
- sides and hips
- rear legs
- tail
- belly feathering
- behind the ears

Be gentle around sensitive areas. Stop if your Golden resists, the skin looks irritated, or brushing causes discomfort.

## Summer Shedding and Heat: What Owners Should Know

Shedding and heat comfort are related, but brushing does not make unsafe heat safe.

A well-maintained coat can support comfort when combined with shade, water, cool rest areas, and careful walk timing. Owners still need a **golden retriever grooming routine** that includes heat planning — not just more brushing on the hottest afternoons.

For walking, hydration, and overheating signs, see [Golden Retriever heat safety](/guides/golden-retriever-heat-safety).

## FAQ

### Why is my Golden Retriever shedding so much in summer?

Golden Retrievers shed heavily because of their double coat, and warm weather often coincides with more loose undercoat coming out. Brushing habits, bathing, indoor climate, health, and diet also play a role. Heavy shedding is usually normal; bald patches, irritated skin, or sudden coat loss deserve a vet check.

### Do Golden Retrievers blow their coat in summer?

Many Goldens shed heavily during seasonal transitions. Some owners describe this as **golden retriever blowing coat**, especially when soft undercoat comes out in clumps. Timing varies by dog, climate, and whether the dog lives mostly indoors.

### How often should I brush my Golden Retriever in summer?

During heavier shedding, several brushings per week are common. Short daily sessions help some dogs more than one long weekly session. Adjust based on how much loose coat you remove and whether mats or skin issues are developing.

### What is the best brush for Golden Retriever summer shedding?

A combination usually works best: a slicker brush for loose coat, an undercoat rake or deshedding tool used carefully for undercoat, and a comb or pin brush for feathering. See [best brushes for Golden Retriever shedding](/guides/best-brushes-golden-retrievers) for specific recommendations.

### Should I shave my Golden Retriever in summer?

Not as a routine solution. Shaving does not stop shedding and can affect coat function and skin protection. Focus on brushing, gentle bathing, and heat-safety habits instead.

### Does bathing help with shedding?

Yes, when done appropriately. Bathing can loosen dead coat and make brushing more effective. It should be paired with thorough drying, gentle shampoo, and regular brushing — not excessive bathing that irritates skin.

### When should I worry about shedding?

Worry when shedding comes with bald patches, red or irritated skin, odor, constant scratching, hot spots, scabs, sudden thinning, or a major change in coat quality. Those signs warrant veterinary attention.

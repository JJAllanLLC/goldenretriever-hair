# Golden Retriever Puppy First Week Home Schedule Chart

## Intro
A consistent routine helps Golden Retriever puppies adjust more quickly to a new home. This **golden retriever puppy first week schedule** chart shows a simple daily structure for feeding, sleep, potty breaks, and play.

---

## Quick Answer

- **Morning:** potty break and breakfast  
- **Late morning:** playtime and short training  
- **Midday:** nap time  
- **Afternoon:** play and potty break  
- **Evening:** dinner, calm play, and wind-down time  
- **Night:** final potty break and bedtime routine

## Why Routines Help New Puppies

- **Reduces anxiety:** predictable patterns help a puppy feel safe in a new environment  
- **Speeds up potty training:** regular trips outside create clear bathroom habits  
- **Improves sleep:** planned naps and bedtimes help puppies settle more easily  
- **Builds good habits:** early structure shapes long-term behavior and expectations  

---

## Tips for the First Week Home

- Keep feeding, sleep, and potty times as **consistent** as possible  
- Limit overwhelming activity and visitors on the first few days  
- Provide **frequent naps** in a crate or pen to prevent overtired meltdowns  
- Introduce training slowly—short sessions, simple cues, lots of rewards

## FAQ

### What should a puppy schedule look like the first week?

It should include regular cycles of **potty → short play or training → nap**, repeated throughout the day, with consistent meal times and a simple bedtime routine.

### How often should an 8-week-old puppy go outside?

An 8-week-old puppy typically needs a potty break **every 30–60 minutes while awake**, plus immediately after waking, eating, and play.

### How much should a new puppy sleep?

Many young Golden Retriever puppies sleep **16–20 hours per day**, spread across nighttime sleep and multiple daytime naps.

### Should I wake my puppy at night?

For the first week or two, you may need to wake your puppy once for a quick overnight potty break, but keep it quiet and businesslike so they go right back to sleep.

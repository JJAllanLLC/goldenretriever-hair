# Golden Retriever Puppy Training Timeline Chart (By Age)

## Intro
Golden Retriever puppies learn best when training follows their developmental stages. This chart shows what skills are typically introduced by age, so you know what to focus on during each phase.

---

## Quick Answer

- **8–10 weeks:** name recognition, crate training  
- **10–12 weeks:** potty training routine, basic commands  
- **3–4 months:** leash walking, sit and stay  
- **4–6 months:** recall training, impulse control  
- **6–12 months:** advanced obedience and socialization

## Why Training Early Matters

- **Brain development:** puppies absorb patterns quickly when training is consistent  
- **Habit formation:** early routines reduce future behavior issues  
- **Socialization window:** young puppies benefit most from positive exposure  
- **Confidence building:** achievable goals prevent frustration and shutdown

---

## Common Puppy Training Mistakes

- Waiting too long to start basic training (then struggling with older habits)  
- Training only when the puppy is already overtired or overexcited  
- Using inconsistent rules between family members  
- Expecting long sessions instead of short, frequent practice

---

## FAQ

### When should I start training a Golden Retriever puppy?

You can start with very short sessions as early as **8 weeks**—focus on crate comfort, name recognition, and gentle basics.

### What commands should a puppy learn first?

Start with practical basics like **name recognition**, **sit**, and **potty routine**. From there, add **stay** and simple leash skills as your puppy grows.

### How long should puppy training sessions be?

Keep sessions short: about **2–5 minutes** for puppies early on, then gradually increase as attention improves.

### When do Golden Retrievers become easier to train?

Many owners notice improvement after adolescence begins to settle—often around **6–18 months**—but consistent, age-appropriate practice matters at every stage.

# Famous Golden Retrievers You May Not Know About

Golden Retrievers are famous for being loving family dogs, but some have also become known for movies, politics, bravery, longevity, and even local leadership. This is a fun roundup of notable Goldens and Golden-related stories from public reporting — not a ranking, just a collection of memorable dogs and why people connected with them.

## Why Some Golden Retrievers Become Famous

Goldens tend to capture attention quickly: expressive faces, a friendly family-dog image, and the kind of trainability that works in public-facing roles. People often project comfort and trust onto them almost instantly, whether the setting is a movie scene, a public event, or a human-interest news story.

They also fit naturally into service-minded narratives. From assistance work to therapy-style visibility, they are often seen as calm, people-focused dogs that can connect emotionally with a wide audience. For the at-home version of that personality, see [Golden Retriever temperament](/guides/golden-retriever-temperament).

## 1. Augie — The Golden Retriever Who Reached 20

Augie (also called August) became widely known in 2020 after turning 20, which is extraordinary for a Golden Retriever. Coverage at the time widely described her as one of the oldest known Golden Retrievers, with some reports calling her the oldest known Golden at that point.

She was adopted later in life and lived with Steve and Jennifer Hetterscheidt in Tennessee, which gave her story extra emotional weight for rescue and senior-dog communities. For many Golden lovers, Augie represented second chances and the deep bond people can build with older dogs. Her story is inspiring, but it should not be read as a guarantee that every Golden can reach that age. ## 2. Liberty — The White House Golden Retriever

Liberty was the Golden Retriever of President Gerald Ford and First Lady Betty Ford. She became one of the most photographed White House pets of the 1970s and was frequently seen in images with President Ford in places like the White House grounds and other official settings.

What made Liberty memorable was the tone she added. In a high-pressure political environment, her presence gave the presidency a more personal, family-dog feel that many people found relatable.

## 3. Buddy — The Golden Retriever from Air Bud

Buddy became widely recognized as the basketball-playing Golden Retriever in *Air Bud*. For a lot of viewers, he helped shape the pop-culture image of the Golden as athletic, clever, and naturally charismatic in family entertainment.

People remember Buddy not just for the sports gimmick, but for the upbeat, feel-good family-movie energy around him. If you want the broader screen-dog context, see [Famous Golden Retrievers in Movies, TV, and Pop Culture](/blog/famous-golden-retrievers-in-movies-tv).

## 4. Comet — The Family Golden from Full House

Comet, the Tanner family’s Golden Retriever on *Full House*, became one of the classic TV family dogs of the 1990s. He was not framed as a stunt-heavy celebrity dog; he was the lovable house dog in a familiar family setting.

That everyday role mattered. Comet helped reinforce the image of the Golden Retriever as a warm, affectionate, “fits right into family life” kind of dog.

## 5. Mayor Max — The Golden Retriever Mayor of Idyllwild

Idyllwild, California is known for a local tradition of Golden Retriever “mayors,” including Mayor Max I, Max II, and Max III in public-facing community roles over time. The role is ceremonial and unofficial because Idyllwild is an unincorporated community, but the tradition has become a real part of the town’s personality.

The tradition is tied to a local election/fundraising concept and grew into a beloved community symbol. People love the story because it is joyful, nonpartisan, and very on-brand for a Golden: friendly, approachable, and good for community spirit.

## 6. Orca — The Brave Assistance Dog

Orca was a Golden Retriever assistance dog in the UK who was publicly recognized for bravery and devotion, including receiving the PDSA Gold Medal. As with many widely shared assistance-dog stories, details are often summarized across news coverage, but the central point is clear: Orca’s work was treated as exceptional and deeply meaningful.

Orca became known for helping his owner during a serious emergency, which is why his story is often discussed as an example of assistance-dog courage and initiative.

Stories like Orca’s remind people that Goldens can be more than cute family pets. In assistance and service-style contexts, their steadiness and people focus can have life-changing impact.

## What These Famous Goldens Have in Common

These stories are very different, but the shared thread is easy to spot: loyalty, friendliness, trainability, emotional connection, and public trust. Whether a Golden becomes a movie icon, a White House companion, a ceremonial town symbol, or an assistance-dog hero, people respond to the same core temperament.

## FAQ

### Who is the most famous Golden Retriever?

Buddy from *Air Bud* may be one of the most famous Golden Retrievers in pop culture, while Liberty, Augie, Orca, and Mayor Max are famous for very different reasons.

### Was Liberty really a Golden Retriever?

Yes. Liberty was the Golden Retriever owned by President Gerald Ford and Betty Ford.

### Was Buddy from Air Bud a Golden Retriever?

Yes. Buddy is widely known as the Golden Retriever star of *Air Bud*.

### What Golden Retriever lived to 20 years old?

Augie, also known as August, became widely known after turning 20 in 2020 and was widely reported as one of the oldest known Golden Retrievers.

### Is Mayor Max a real mayor?

Mayor Max is best understood as a ceremonial or unofficial dog mayor tied to Idyllwild’s local tradition and fundraiser-style election, not a standard government office.

### Can Golden Retrievers be service or assistance dogs?

Yes. Golden Retrievers are often used in assistance, therapy, and service-style roles because of their trainability, people-focused nature, and steady temperament. Orca is one notable example of the impact that work can have.
